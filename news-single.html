Covid-19: Kenya records highest number of recoveries yet
John Maina

June 13, 2020

Kenya, Tuesday, announced the recovery of 587 people from Covid-19, the highest number of recoveries in the country since its first positive case on March 13.

This took the total number of patients to have recovered from the virus to 9,327. Of the new recoveries, 500 patients were on home-based care while 87 were admitted in various hospitals across the country, Health Chief Administrative Secretary Rashid Aman said during the daily update.

However, the country recorded an additional 605 positive cases of the Coronavirus raising the national caseload to 23,202. Of the 605 new patients, 528 are Kenyans and 27 foreigners, the youngest a two-months-old infant and the oldest 99. The new cases were derived from 4,547 samples. The country has so far tested a total of 322,923 samples for the new Coronavirus.
